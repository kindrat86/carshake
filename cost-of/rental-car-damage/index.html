What Does Rental Car Damage Actually Cost You?

Rental car companies charge more than just the body shop bill. They add loss-of-use fees (what they would have earned renting the car while it was being repaired), diminished value, and administrative fees. A $400 scratch can become a $900 charge.

Body shop repair cost

$300–$2,000

The actual repair bill — same as any body shop estimate. The rental company does not mark up the repair; they pass through the body shop invoice.

Loss-of-use fee

$30–$60/day

The rental company charges you for each day the car is unavailable for rental while being repaired. For a 5-day repair, that adds $150–$300.

Diminished value

$100–$500

If the damage is reported to Carfax or a similar service, the car's resale value drops. Rental companies may charge you for this loss of value.

Administrative fee

$50–$150

A processing fee for handling the claim, taking photos, coordinating with the body shop, and processing your payment.

Total for a minor scrape

$600–$900

A $400 repair plus $200 in loss-of-use, $100 admin and $100 diminished value. The total is often double the repair cost.

Is the collision damage waiver (CDW) worth it?

At $15–$30/day, the CDW is expensive insurance for a single risk. If your personal auto policy or credit card provides rental coverage, you can usually decline the CDW — but always verify your specific coverage before you travel.

What if the rental company charges me for damage I didn't cause?

Request their evidence. If they cannot produce a dated, timestamped intake or return inspection report showing the damage, dispute the charge. A documented walk-around at pickup and return (timestamped photos) is your best defense.
