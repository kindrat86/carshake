Condition timeline: definition

A condition timeline is the chronological sequence of condition records for a single vehicle — every timestamped scan, in order. Its value is subtraction: compare any two points on the timeline and you know exactly what changed between them, and who had the car when it did.

Condition timeline, explained

A single condition record proves what a car looked like at one moment. A timeline turns those moments into custody windows. If the car was scanned at valet drop-off at 7:14 pm and again at pick-up at 10:52 pm, any damage present in the second scan but not the first appeared inside that window — while the vehicle was in the valet's care. No memory, no goodwill, no argument required.

What a timeline entry contains

In CarShake, each entry is a full scan: 8 photographed angles captured in about 60 seconds, each photo GPS-stamped, server-timestamped, and SHA-256 hashed, with a QR handshake confirmation from the other party at handover. Entries accumulate automatically as the vehicle is scanned at successive handovers.

Example

  1. Mon 08:30 — fleet check-out scan, driver confirms. Clean nearside doors.
  2. Wed 17:45 — check-in scan. Scrape on nearside rear door.
  3. Conclusion — the scrape occurred during that driver's custody window. The comparison view highlights it; the PDF evidence report documents it.

Who relies on condition timelines

Fleet managers use them to assign accountability across drivers, rental companies to settle return disputes, and individual drivers to prove a valet or garage claim. The underlying evidence standard is described under damage proof.

Frequently asked questions

How is a condition timeline different from a vehicle history report?

A history report (like Carfax) aggregates past events — accidents, title changes, service records. A condition timeline documents physical condition at specific handovers, with photos and timestamps.

How many scans do I need for a useful timeline?

Two is enough to bound one custody window. Regular per-handover scans make every window provable.

Can both parties see the timeline entries?

Each handover record is confirmed by both parties via QR handshake, so both sides hold the same evidence for that entry.
