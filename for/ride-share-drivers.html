The damage risk for rideshare drivers

Rideshare drivers face unique vehicle damage risks. Every passenger that enters your car is a potential source of interior damage: stained seats, scratched door panels, broken cup holders, and torn floor mats. Over a typical 8-hour shift, you might carry 15-30 passengers, any one of whom could cause damage you won't notice until the next rider points it out or your weekly inspection reveals it.

Without documented condition between rides, rideshare drivers have no recourse when passengers cause damage. Uber and Lyft's damage reporting windows are tight (typically 48 hours), and without timestamped photos showing the car was undamaged before a specific trip, claims get denied.

How CarShake protects rideshare drivers

CarShake lets you document your vehicle condition before each shift in about 90 seconds. If a passenger causes damage, you have proof it wasn't there before that trip. For drivers running premium vehicles on Uber Black or Lyft Lux, this documentation is essential for protecting expensive interiors.

The protocol takes about 90 seconds:

  1. Scan before handover: Walk around the vehicle capturing all exterior panels, wheels, and interior. CarShake timestamps and geolocates every photo.
  2. QR receipt at handover: Generate a QR code that the customer, passenger, or driver scans, creating a cryptographic receipt linking them to the documented condition.
  3. Scan at return: When the vehicle comes back, repeat the scan. Any new damage is flagged and attributable to the specific custody period.

Frequently asked questions

How much does CarShake cost for rideshare drivers?

Can customers dispute CarShake documentation?

CarShake photos include cryptographic timestamps and geolocation data that are extremely difficult to forge. Credit card companies and courts accept this documentation as strong evidence of vehicle condition at a specific time and place, which significantly reduces successful disputes.

Does this work with my existing rental or fleet management software?

CarShake offers API integrations with popular rental and fleet management platforms. Condition reports can flow directly into your existing dashboard alongside rental agreements and vehicle records.

What if a customer refuses to scan the QR code?

The QR scan is optional. Your pre-handover photos are the critical evidence. If a customer won't scan the QR, you still have timestamped proof of the vehicle's condition before they took possession, which is usually sufficient for dispute resolution.
