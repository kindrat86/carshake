Insurance adjusters know that valet and parking damage claims are among the hardest to adjudicate — they hinge entirely on when damage occurred. CarShake provides a tamper-evident, timestamped before-and-after record that answers that question definitively. Every scan is GPS-tagged and stored immutably, so adjusters can verify the exact location and time of each photo. For claims involving CarShake-verified handovers, adjusters can close files faster, with fewer disputes and less back-and-forth. The QR handover receipt adds a second layer of confirmation, showing that both the driver and the valet attendant acknowledged the vehicle condition at drop-off.

For adjusters managing high claim volumes, the CarShake evidence format also speeds up triage: claims with a verified before-and-after comparison can be processed in a single review session, rather than requiring multiple rounds of documentation requests and follow-up calls. The result is a faster claims cycle, lower administrative cost, and better customer satisfaction scores for the carrier. Adjusters who encounter a CarShake QR-referenced claim for the first time should look for four things: the timestamp on each scan, the GPS coordinates matching the reported location, the QR handover receipt showing both-party confirmation, and the AI-generated comparison flagging specific areas of new damage. These four data points together answer the only question that matters: did this damage occur while the vehicle was in the valet custody.
