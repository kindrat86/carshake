How Los Angeles valet damage law works

California Civil Code section 2985 and the California Vehicle Code impose strict requirements on valet operators. California is a comparative negligence state.

In practice, this means the valet company is not automatically liable. You have to prove the damage occurred while they had control of your vehicle. That requires evidence: photos taken immediately before handover, a timestamped receipt, and ideally photos at pickup showing any new damage. Most Los Angeles drivers do not have this evidence, which is exactly why valet damage claims get denied.
