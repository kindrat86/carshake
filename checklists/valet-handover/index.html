The Ultimate Valet Handover Checklist: 10 Things to Do Before Handing Over Your Keys

Three minutes. Ten actions. Zero damage disputes. Follow this checklist every time you hand your car to a valet, and you'll have the evidence you need if something goes wrong.

1

Open CarShake on your phone

Go to carshake.online in any phone browser. No app download, no signup. The camera launches automatically when you start a scan.

2

Run the 60-second 8-angle scan

Walk around your car. CarShake captures 8 timestamped, GPS-verified, SHA-256 hashed photos covering every exterior angle — front, rear, both sides, and all four wheel corners. Takes about 60 seconds.

3

Show the QR code to the attendant

CarShake generates a QR-coded handover receipt after your scan. Show it to the valet attendant — they scan it to digitally acknowledge the vehicle's condition at the moment of handover. This creates a mutual digital handshake.

4

Note the attendant's name

Write down the valet attendant's name or badge number. If something goes wrong, you need to know who handled your car.

5

Keep the valet ticket

The ticket proves the bailment existed — without it, the valet can claim they never took custody of your car. Photograph it immediately as a backup. The ticket number is referenced in your demand letter.

6

Photograph the surroundings

Snap a photo of the valet stand, the parking area, and any posted signs. Note the location of any security cameras — they may have captured the incident.

7

Note the exact time

Record the precise handover time. Combined with GPS data, timestamps establish the exact start of the bailment custody window. This is what ties damage to their watch.

8

Remove visible valuables

Valet tickets often disclaim liability for personal property. Remove sunglasses, electronics, bags, and anything visible from the cabin. While not the valet's primary liability, it eliminates a secondary dispute.

9

Inspect before driving away (critical!)

Do not drive away until you've inspected your car. Walk around. Check the bumpers, doors, mirrors, wheels, and rear. Compare with your mental image from the pre-scan. The moment you leave, the valet can claim the damage happened after you left.

10

If damage: rescan immediately with CarShake

If you discover damage, open CarShake and run a second scan immediately — before leaving the scene. The AI comparison flags every new scratch, dent, or scrape. You now have irrefutable before/after evidence, timestamped and GPS-verified. Show the comparison to the valet manager and demand an incident report.
