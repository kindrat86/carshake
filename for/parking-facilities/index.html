CarShake for Parking Facilities

CarShake gives garages and lots a timestamped record of a vehicle's condition at entry. When a customer claims their car was damaged while parked, the question becomes a comparison of records, not a negotiation about goodwill.

Why parking operators use CarShake

Damage claims are a standing cost for parking facilities. Some are legitimate; many involve damage that existed before the car ever entered the ramp. Without entry-condition evidence, operators face an unpleasant choice: pay claims they doubt, or fight them and lose customers. Camera footage rarely settles it — coverage has gaps, and footage shows events, not condition.

CarShake documents condition directly: an 8-angle scan at entry, about 60 seconds, with GPS stamps, server timestamps, and SHA-256 hashes, confirmed by the customer via QR handshake. For attended facilities the attendant runs it; for self-park, signage invites customers to scan for their own protection — which protects you equally, because the record cuts both ways.

Where it fits your operation

It suits valet-assisted garages, monthly-parker onboarding, airport long-term lots, and any facility whose insurer asks what evidence backs a claim denial. See also protecting your car at airport parking for the driver's side of the same flow.

How a CarShake handover works

  1. Scan before handover. A guided walk-around captures 8 angles of the vehicle in about 60 seconds. Every photo is GPS-stamped, server-timestamped, and SHA-256 hashed, so nobody can backdate or alter it.
  2. Confirm with a QR handshake. The other party scans a QR code and acknowledges the recorded condition. Both sides now hold the same evidence.
  3. Re-scan on return. A second walk-around captures the vehicle's condition at hand-back.
  4. Compare. AI-assisted comparison lines up the before and after scans and flags anything new. If there is a dispute, either side can export a court-ready PDF evidence report.

Frequently asked questions

Our facility is self-park. Who does the scan?

The customer does, from their own phone browser — no app install. A posted QR code starts the flow. Their record protects both sides.

Does this replace our CCTV?

No. CCTV shows events in covered areas; CarShake documents vehicle condition at a point in time. Together they answer both halves of a claim.

What do we show an insurer or small-claims court?

The PDF evidence report: photos of all angles with their GPS and server timestamps.
