CarShake for Car Dealerships

CarShake gives dealerships a 60-second condition record everywhere a car changes hands: service drop-offs, loaner cars, test drives, and trade-in appraisals. Each scan is timestamped and confirmed by the customer, so “that scratch wasn't there this morning” conversations end with evidence.

Why dealerships use CarShake

A dealership hands vehicles back and forth all day. A customer drops a car for service and picks it up swearing the door ding is new. A loaner comes back with a cracked bumper nobody will own. A trade-in's condition at appraisal gets disputed at final signing a week later. Every one of these is a handover without a shared record.

CarShake makes the record part of the handover itself. The service writer scans the car at drop-off and the customer confirms it with a QR handshake — about 60 seconds, on any phone, no app install. The same flow covers the loaner going out and coming back.

Where it fits your operation

Service lane: scan at drop-off, re-scan at delivery, and disputes about wash-bay scratches disappear. Loaners: both directions of every loan are documented. Test drives: a quick scan protects you on high-value inventory. Trade-ins: the appraisal-day scan fixes the car's condition on the date the number was quoted.

How a CarShake handover works

  1. Scan before handover. A guided walk-around captures 8 angles of the vehicle in about 60 seconds. Every photo is GPS-stamped, server-timestamped, and SHA-256 hashed, so nobody can backdate or alter it.
  2. Confirm with a QR handshake. The other party scans a QR code and acknowledges the recorded condition. Both sides now hold the same evidence.
  3. Re-scan on return. A second walk-around captures the vehicle's condition at hand-back.
  4. Compare. AI-assisted comparison lines up the before and after scans and flags anything new. If there is a dispute, either side can export a court-ready PDF evidence report.

Frequently asked questions

Does this replace our DMS or service-lane inspection tools?

No — it complements them. CarShake is the customer-facing condition record with mutual confirmation; your DMS keeps being the system of record for the work itself.

How long does a scan take in the service lane?

About 60 seconds for the guided 8-angle walk-around. Advisors typically scan while greeting the customer.

What does the customer see?

They scan a QR code in their phone browser, review the captured condition, and confirm. Both sides can retrieve the record later.
