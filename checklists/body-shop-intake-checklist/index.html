Body Shop Intake Checklist: Document Every Panel Before Repair

Body shops handle cars that are already damaged — which makes it critical to document what is pre-existing versus what is repair-related. This checklist protects your shop from false claims.

  1. Photograph every panel: front, rear, both sides, hood, roof, trunk, all four wheels
  2. Circle and photograph all pre-existing damage on a printed diagram (or digital equivalent):Circle and photograph all pre-existing damage on a printed diagram (or digital equivalent)
  3. Have the customer sign the intake form acknowledging the documented condition:Have the customer sign the intake form acknowledging the documented condition
  4. Note the odometer reading and fuel level:Note the odometer reading and fuel level
  5. Photograph the interior: dashboard, seats, cargo area
  6. Generate a CarShake QR handover receipt for a timestamped, immutable record both parties can access:Generate a CarShake QR handover receipt for a timestamped, immutable record both parties can access
  7. Re-document at completion: same panels, same angles, showing the repaired areas
  8. Email the before-and-after package to the customer — it is both quality assurance and legal protection:Email the before-and-after package to the customer — it is both quality assurance and legal protection
  9. Store the intake records for at least 90 days after the repair is completed:Store the intake records for at least 90 days after the repair is completed

Is this checklist legally binding?

A signed, timestamped intake form with photos is strong evidence in a dispute. Coupled with a CarShake QR handover receipt, it creates a contemporaneous evidence chain that courts recognize.

What if a customer refuses to sign?

Note the refusal on the intake form, take your photos anyway, and have a witness initial the refusal note. A documented refusal is better than no record at all.
