CarShake vs Inspectr

Consumer valet damage proof vs. enterprise fleet inspection — which fits your job?

Inspectr is a vehicle inspection platform built for dealerships, fleet operators, and professional inspectors. It leans on multi-point checklists, OBD-II and hardware integrations, and per-seat enterprise pricing — the right tool when a technician needs to log a 150-point mechanical and cosmetic condition report on a vehicle entering or leaving inventory. CarShake is built for the other end of the journey: the moment a driver hands the keys to a valet, rental agent, or mechanic, and wants court-admissible proof of what the car looked like 60 seconds earlier.

The two products overlap on one word — "inspection" — and almost nothing else. One serves an enterprise procurement process; the other serves a consumer who has 90 seconds at a valet stand and a phone in their pocket. Below is an honest side-by-side so you can pick the right tool for the job rather than the louder marketing page.

When to choose CarShake

Pick CarShake if you are the person handing the keys over — not the person logging inventory. The whole product is tuned for a 60-second walk-around: open the app, follow the on-screen angle prompts, hand the valet a QR-coded receipt, and walk away with a timestamped record you can produce months later if a scratch shows up on your bumper. There is no checklist to configure, no seat to provision, and no procurement form to fill out.

CarShake also wins when the dispute is the point. A dealership inspection report describes condition; a CarShake handover receipt proves who held the car when. That distinction matters in small-claims court, with a rental desk that wants to charge you for pre-existing damage, or with a valet operator whose insurer is pushing back. The evidence chain is designed to be admissible, not just descriptive.

When Inspectr might be better

Inspectr is the stronger choice if you are running a service lane, a wholesale floor, or a fleet depot and you need standardized mechanical inspections across dozens of technicians. Its checklist engine, VIN decoding, OBD-II pull, and DMS integration solve an operational problem CarShake does not touch. If your day starts with "log 40 incoming trade-ins" rather than "prove my car was already scratched," Inspectr's workflow is built for that volume.

Inspectr is also a better fit inside an enterprise stack where procurement requires SSO, audit exports, and a named account team. CarShake is a self-serve consumer tool; if your buying committee needs SOC 2 documentation and a master services agreement, the enterprise inspection platform is the realistic path.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use CarShake and Inspectr together?

Yes. A dealership can run Inspectr for inventory intake and recommend CarShake to customers at the service-drive handover. They cover different moments in the vehicle's life.

Does CarShake replace Inspectr's mechanical inspection?

No. CarShake documents cosmetic condition and custody at handover; it does not pull OBD-II codes or run mechanical diagnostics.

Is CarShake's free tier enough, or do I need Shield+?

The free tier covers three scans per month, which suits an occasional valet or rental user. Shield+ ($2.97/month) unlocks unlimited scans, PDF reports, and priority support.

Will an Inspectr report hold up in a damage dispute?

Inspectr reports document condition but are not built around a mutual handover receipt, so they describe rather than prove custody. CarShake's QR-confirmed receipt is designed specifically for that evidentiary gap.
