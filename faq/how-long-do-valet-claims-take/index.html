How long do valet damage claims take?

Most valet damage claims resolve in 2-6 weeks. Court-admissible timestamped evidence (like CarShake) is the single biggest factor in faster resolution.

Detailed answer

Most valet damage claims resolve in two to six weeks when you present timestamped evidence upfront, but the timeline depends heavily on three factors: whether the operator carries insurance, the quality of your documentation, and how aggressively the operator's insurer disputes the claim. Undocumented disputes involving uninsured operators routinely drag on for three to six months, while well-documented claims against insured operators often settle in under three weeks.

The fastest variable you control is the evidence. When you submit a complete package — timestamped pre-handover photos, a QR handover receipt, post-retrieval photos, two repair estimates, and a written incident report — the operator's insurer has very little room to dispute liability. Most will tender an offer within 14 to 21 days. CarShake's three-stop protocol produces most of this package automatically, which is why documented claims resolve dramatically faster than undocumented ones.

The slowest variable is the operator. Independent valet operators at restaurants and small venues frequently carry no garage-keepers liability coverage at all. When the operator is uninsured, your own auto insurer becomes the primary recovery path. Your insurer will typically pay your collision claim within 7 to 14 days, then pursue subrogation against the operator for the payout and your deductible. Subrogation can take months, but you get paid regardless.

How to speed up your claim

  • Submit your demand in writing within 24 hours of the damage
  • Include timestamped pre- and post-handover scans from CarShake
  • Provide two independent repair estimates
  • File with both the valet's insurer and your own simultaneously
  • Send a complaint to your state's department of insurance if the claim stalls past 30 days

Related questions

Why is my claim taking so long?

The most common cause is incomplete documentation. Insurers use missing records as a reason to delay. Submit everything in a single package and request written acknowledgment of receipt.

What happens if the valet's insurer denies my claim?

File with your own auto insurer for subrogation, file a complaint with the state department of insurance, and consider small claims court for damages up to $5,000–$10,000.

Will filing affect my insurance rates?

Not-at-fault claims typically do not trigger surcharges, but check your policy's surcharge schedule. Filing with the at-fault party's insurer first preserves your options.

Can I get a rental car while the claim is pending?

If your policy includes rental reimbursement, yes. Otherwise, request loss-of-use compensation from the valet's insurer as part of your demand.
