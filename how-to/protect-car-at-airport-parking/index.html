How to Protect Your Car at Airport Parking: The Complete Airport Parking Guide

Quick answer: Airport parking lots handle thousands of vehicles daily, cars sit unattended for days, and damage claims are among the hardest to win because you are not present when the damage occurs. Here is how to protect your car before, during, and after your trip.

Published 2026-07-09

Step 1: Document your car's condition before you leave

Use CarShake's 8-angle AI scan at the airport parking lot BEFORE you board your shuttle or walk to the terminal. The GPS stamp proves you were at the lot, and the timestamp proves the car was undamaged at drop-off.

Step 2: Choose your parking spot strategically

If possible, park in an end spot or a spot next to a pillar — this reduces the number of adjacent vehicles. Avoid spots near cart returns, tight corners, or high-traffic pedestrian paths. In garage parking, upper levels typically have fewer vehicles and lower turnover.

Step 3: Remove valuables and use a sunshade

Visible valuables attract break-ins, and a sunshade signals that the car is attended to (even if it isn't). Both are low-effort risk reducers.

Step 4: Re-scan immediately upon return

Before you put your luggage in the trunk, walk around the car and scan again. Compare to your pre-trip scan. Any new damage that occurred while you were away will be visible instantly. Report it to the lot office before leaving the property — the same rule applies: once you drive off, your leverage drops dramatically.

Does airport parking insurance cover damage?

Most parking lot liability policies cover damage caused by the lot's own operations (e.g., a shuttle bus hitting your car). They typically do NOT cover damage caused by other patrons. Your personal auto policy's comprehensive coverage would apply, subject to your deductible.

What if my car is damaged and the lot has no cameras?

Your timestamped before-and-after CarShake scans are your evidence — they prove where and when the damage occurred, with or without security footage. GPS coordinates embedded in the scan tie it to the lot's location.
