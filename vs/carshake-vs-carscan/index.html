CarShake vs Carscan

First, the thing everyone Googles: CarShake and Carscan are two different products with similar names. If you're evaluating dealer/insurer inspection software, you want Carscan. If you want proof your car was already scratched when you handed over the keys, you want CarShake.

Not the same company. Carscan (carscan.ai) is an AI vehicle-inspection and valuation platform built for dealerships, insurers, and workshops. CarShake (carshake.online) is a consumer app for valet, rental, and service-drive damage disputes. Similar name, different job.

Carscan is a trade-grade inspection and valuation platform. By its own description it uses AI and computer vision trained on 2 million-plus vehicle images to scan a car's exterior, wheels, and glass, read internal data like odometer and dashboard warnings, match VIN to licence plate for fraud detection, and even pull real-time repair quotes from local workshops — with AR guidance that completes the walk-around in about 30 seconds. It serves four buyers: consumers, dealers, insurers, and workshops, and sells through a contact-sales process (its listed contact is based in South Africa).

CarShake does far less on paper, on purpose. It's built for one moment: the 90 seconds when a driver hands the keys to a valet, rental agent, or mechanic and wants court-admissible proof of what the car looked like beforehand. No procurement, no repair marketplace, no VIN fraud engine — just scan, receipt, done.

When to choose CarShake

Pick CarShake if you're the person handing over the keys. The entire product is tuned for a 90-second walk-around: open the app, follow the angle prompts, hand the valet a QR-coded receipt, and walk away with a timestamped record you can produce months later if a scratch appears. The QR handover receipt is the part that matters in a dispute — it proves not just condition but custody: who held the car, and when. That's the evidentiary gap a rental desk or valet insurer argues over, and it's exactly what a general inspection report doesn't capture. Pricing is public and self-serve, so there's no sales call to book before you're protected tonight.

When Carscan is the better fit

Carscan is the stronger choice if you're on the trade side. If you run a dealership, an insurance claims desk, or a workshop and you need standardized condition assessment, valuation, VIN-to-plate fraud checks, and repair estimates at inventory scale, CarShake simply doesn't operate there. Carscan's computer-vision model, internal-data reads, and workshop-quote marketplace solve an operational and commercial problem — pricing a car and routing a repair — that a consumer custody-proof app is not built to touch. If your day is "assess and value 40 incoming vehicles," Carscan is built for that; if your day is "prove my car was fine when I parked it," CarShake is.

Frequently asked questions

Are CarShake and Carscan the same product?

No — two different products with similar-sounding names. Carscan (carscan.ai) is an AI vehicle-inspection and valuation platform for dealers, insurers, and workshops. CarShake is a consumer app that creates court-admissible proof of a car's condition at handover.

Which fights a valet or rental damage charge?

CarShake. It's purpose-built for consumer damage disputes: a guided 8-angle scan plus a QR handover receipt proving who held the car and when. Carscan focuses on trade-side assessment, valuation, and repair quotes.

Does Carscan have consumer pricing like CarShake?

Carscan uses a contact-sales model and doesn't publish consumer pricing. CarShake is self-serve: free for 3 scans/month, or $2.97/month for unlimited on Shield+.

Can a dealership use both?

Yes. A dealer could use Carscan for inventory assessment and valuation and still recommend CarShake to customers at the service-drive handover — they cover different moments in the vehicle's life.
