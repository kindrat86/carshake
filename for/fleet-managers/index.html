CarShake for Fleet Managers

CarShake gives fleets a condition record at every check-out and check-in, so unreported damage can be traced to the handover where it appeared instead of being absorbed as “nobody knows.” Each vehicle accumulates a timestamped condition timeline.

Why fleet managers use CarShake

In most fleets, damage surfaces at the worst time — at resale inspection, at lease return, or when the next driver refuses the vehicle. By then, five drivers have used the car and none of them did it. Without per-handover records, the cost lands on the fleet budget and driver accountability is a fiction.

CarShake makes the walk-around scan part of check-out and check-in: about 60 seconds, 8 angles, GPS-stamped and server-timestamped, confirmed by the driver with a QR handshake. Damage that appears between two scans belongs to exactly one custody window. Drivers scan more carefully when they know the record is real — which is precisely the point.

Where it fits your operation

It covers motor pools, assigned vehicles at periodic intervals, grey-fleet handovers, and lease returns. Because every scan is stored with its timestamps, each vehicle builds a condition timeline you can consult before resale or end-of-lease negotiations.

How a CarShake handover works

  1. Scan before handover. A guided walk-around captures 8 angles of the vehicle in about 60 seconds. Every photo is GPS-stamped, server-timestamped, and SHA-256 hashed, so nobody can backdate or alter it.
  2. Confirm with a QR handshake. The other party scans a QR code and acknowledges the recorded condition. Both sides now hold the same evidence.
  3. Re-scan on return. A second walk-around captures the vehicle's condition at hand-back.
  4. Compare. AI-assisted comparison lines up the before and after scans and flags anything new. If there is a dispute, either side can export a court-ready PDF evidence report.

Frequently asked questions

Do drivers need to install anything?

No. CarShake runs in the phone browser. Drivers scan a QR code, do the guided walk-around, and confirm.

How does this help at lease return?

You arrive with a timestamped history of the vehicle's condition rather than debating the lessor's inspection line by line.

Can I require scans only for pool vehicles?

Yes — the workflow is per-handover, so you decide which vehicles and events require a scan.
