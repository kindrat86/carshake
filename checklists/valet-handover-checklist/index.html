Complete valet parking handover checklist

This checklist covers every step you should take when handling vehicle documentation at a valet. Following it systematically protects you against damage disputes, liability claims, and undocumented charges.

Step-by-step checklist

  1. Scan exterior with CarShake before handing over keys
  2. Photograph interior and dashboard
  3. Remove valuables or place in trunk
  4. Set odometer reading on the QR receipt
  5. Confirm valet ticket matches vehicle description
  6. Verify the valet company name on the ticket
  7. Note any pre-existing damage on the ticket
  8. At pickup, scan again before leaving the venue

Why each step matters

Skip a step, and you create a gap in your documentation that can be exploited later. For example, if you do not photograph the odometer at drop-off, a valet can claim excessive mileage. If you do not scan the condition at handover, pre-existing damage may be attributed to you.

CarShake automates the critical photo-documentation steps. Instead of manually managing photos and notes, the app walks you through each panel and timestamp, ensuring nothing is missed.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Rushing through inspection: Take the full 5-10 minutes. Rushing leads to missed damage.
  • Signing without inspecting: Never sign a condition acknowledgment without verifying every detail.
  • Skipping photos of minor damage: Small scratches become major disputes. Document everything.
  • Not getting signatures: Verbal agreements are not evidence. Get written acknowledgment.
  • Forgetting the interior: Interior damage is frequently disputed. Photograph seats, dash, and cargo areas.

Pro tips from claims adjusters

  • Use CarShake's timestamped photos as your primary evidence layer.
  • Always get the name and contact info of the person receiving your vehicle.
  • Keep copies of all paperwork, originals get lost.
  • Follow up any verbal communication with an email summary.
  • If damage appears at pickup, document it before leaving the valet.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a proper inspection take?

Plan for 5-10 minutes for a thorough inspection. CarShake's guided workflow helps you cover all critical areas systematically in about 90 seconds, with additional time for noting any existing damage.

What if the valet staff will not wait for me to inspect?

Insist. Any reputable valet will accommodate a reasonable inspection. If they refuse, consider it a red flag and document the situation. Take dated photos anyway, even if staff will not co-sign.

Do I need to photograph every panel?

Yes. Disputes often center on specific panels, and without photos of all of them, the other party can claim damage existed elsewhere. CarShake makes comprehensive capture quick and guided.

Can I use this checklist for insurance purposes?

Absolutely. Insurance adjusters rely on documented condition reports. CarShake photos with timestamps and geolocation are particularly compelling evidence for claims and disputes.
