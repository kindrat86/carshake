Rental Car Inspection Checklist

Before you drive a rental off the lot, photograph every body panel, the roof, all four wheels, the windshield, and the interior — at least 12 timestamped photos in one slow walk-around — and make the agent record every existing scratch on the rental agreement before you sign it. That 5-minute routine is what separates a zero-dollar dispute from a surprise damage bill weeks later. The full checklist, in order, is below.

What should I check before leaving the rental lot?

Work around the car in one direction so you skip nothing: exterior panels, glass and lights, wheels, interior, then paperwork. Photograph first, argue later — a dated photo of a pre-existing scratch ends most disputes before they start.

Exterior walk-around

  • Front bumper and grille — look low: parking-block scrapes hide under the lip
  • Hood and roof — hail dents and sap show best at an angle
  • Both front doors, both rear doors, and both fenders — run a finger over suspect marks; a chip you can feel is damage, not dirt
  • Side mirrors, housings and glass — mirror scuffs are among the most commonly billed items
  • Rear bumper, trunk lid and tailgate — check the bumper's top surface where luggage drags

Glass, lights and wipers

  • Windshield: scan for chips smaller than a fingernail — they spread, and you'll be blamed for the crack
  • All lights on: headlights, brake lights, indicators — a cracked lens counts as damage
  • Wiper blades intact and seated

Wheels, tires and underside

  • All 4 rims — curb rash is the single most disputed rental item; shoot each wheel straight-on
  • Tire tread and sidewalls — bulges or plugs mean ask for another car
  • Spare tire and jack present (check the trunk floor)

Interior and dashboard

  • Seats, carpets and headliner — stains, burns, tears
  • Dashboard warning lights with the ignition on — photograph the cluster showing fuel level and mileage in one frame
  • Infotainment screen undamaged; delete your data before returning

Paperwork — the step most renters skip

Every mark you found goes on the rental agreement's damage diagram before you sign. If the agent says "don't worry about small ones," insist anyway — verbal assurances don't survive a billing department. Keep your copy until the final credit-card statement clears, typically 60 days after return.

When you return the car

Repeat the same walk-around in the same order, ideally at the same time of day. If no agent inspects the car at drop-off, photograph it parked in the return lane with signage visible. Damage claims filed days after an unattended return are the classic scenario our dispute guide covers.

Do photos really hold up in a dispute?

Contemporaneous, timestamped photos are the strongest evidence a renter can produce. Plain camera-roll photos work; their weakness is provable timing and location.
