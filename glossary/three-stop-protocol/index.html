Three-stop protocol, explained

The three-stop protocol is CarShake's core workflow for producing court-admissible vehicle condition evidence. It is named for the three discrete moments at which condition is recorded: before handover, at the moment of handover, and after retrieval. Each stop produces a specific type of evidence that, taken together, form an unbroken chain of custody.

The first stop is the pre-handover scan. Before you give your keys to a valet, attendant, or rental agent, you walk around the vehicle while CarShake captures a guided set of timestamped, geo-located photographs. The second stop is the QR handover receipt: at the exact moment custody transfers, CarShake generates a QR code that links to the pre-handover scan and stamps the time of transfer. The third stop is the post-retrieval scan, performed when the vehicle is returned. CarShake automatically compares it against the pre-handover scan and flags any new damage.

The protocol is designed around the evidentiary standard courts and insurers actually apply. A single "before" photo or a single "after" photo proves nothing in isolation. What matters is an unbroken timeline anchored by tamper-evident timestamps from a neutral third party. The three-stop protocol produces that timeline automatically, with no user expertise required.

Why three-stop protocol matters

Valet damage disputes almost always come down to one question: did the damage exist before handover, or did it occur during the operator's custody? Without a documented timeline, that question is unanswerable, and the operator's insurer wins by default. The three-stop protocol converts a he-said-she-said dispute into a documented chain of evidence that the operator cannot credibly dispute.

The protocol also matters because it is built for non-experts. Anyone can run a scan in under 60 seconds without training, which means the evidence gets captured consistently regardless of who is using the app. The result is a tamper-evident record that holds up in small claims court, in insurance arbitrations, and in formal litigation.

How CarShake handles this

CarShake encodes the three-stop protocol directly into the app's primary workflow. A pre-handover scan, a QR handover receipt, and a post-retrieval scan are each one tap. The app guides the user through each step and refuses to let a handover complete without the preceding scan. Server-side timestamps make the records tamper-evident, and the linked chain is exportable as a single PDF evidence file.

Frequently asked questions

What is three-stop protocol?

CarShake's three-stop protocol is the workflow of recording vehicle condition at three specific moments — before handover, at handover, and after retrieval — to produce an unbroken chain of custody.

How long does a full three-stop cycle take?

About 90 seconds end to end. The pre-handover scan is roughly 45 seconds, the QR receipt is instant, and the post-retrieval scan is another 45 seconds.

Can I use only one or two of the three stops?

You can, but the chain of custody is weaker. The protocol is designed to be used as a complete set; missing any stop creates a gap the operator's insurer can exploit.
