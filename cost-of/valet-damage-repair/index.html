What Does Valet Damage Repair Actually Cost?

Valet damage repair costs range from under $150 for a buffable scratch to over $4,200 for panel replacement and paint matching. But the number you actually pay depends on one thing: whether you can prove the damage occurred in the valet's custody.

Minor surface scratch (buff-out)

$100–$200

A professional detailer can often buff out light clear-coat scratches in 20 minutes. If this is all the damage, pay cash — it is cheaper than your deductible.

Deep scratch through paint

$400–$800

Requires sanding, priming, painting, and clear-coat. Most body shops have a minimum charge ($300–$500 per panel) regardless of scratch size.

Door ding / small dent

$150–$500

Paintless dent repair (PDR) can remove small dents for $150–$300 if the paint is intact. If the paint is cracked, it becomes a body-shop job at $400+.

Bumper scrape / replacement

$600–$2,000

A scraped bumper cover may need replacement rather than repair. Modern bumpers with sensors and cameras make this the most expensive common valet repair.

Panel dent with paint damage

$1,200–$2,500

A dented door or quarter panel that needs body work, painting, and blending into adjacent panels. Two panels damaged (e.g., door + quarter) doubles the cost.

Front splitter / underbody damage

$1,500–$4,200

Low front splitters on sports cars are the most expensive valet damage — they scrape on ramps and speed bumps, and replacement often requires OEM parts from the manufacturer.

Does insurance cover valet damage?

Comprehensive coverage typically applies, but you pay your deductible ($500–$1,000). If the repair cost is under your deductible, you pay everything out of pocket and filing a claim may still raise your premium.

Can I make the valet company pay?

Yes — if you have timestamped evidence of the car's condition at handover. Without it, the valet will dispute that the damage occurred in their custody, and you will likely pay.
