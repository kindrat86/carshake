The Complete Rental Car Inspection Checklist: Never Pay for Pre-Existing Damage

Rental companies charge thousands of customers every year for damage they did not cause. The only protection is a documented walk-around with timestamped photos at pickup AND return.

  1. Front bumper: photograph any scrapes, license plate bolts, and lower lip
  2. Rear bumper: check for parking scrapes, especially on the corners
  3. All four wheels and tires: photograph rim rash, curb damage, and tire sidewall condition
  4. Driver side doors and panels: open each door and check the edges for chips
  5. Passenger side doors and panels: same inspection, including door handles
  6. Hood: check for dents, paint chips, and stone damage
  7. Roof: look for dents from luggage or low-clearance garages
  8. Trunk: open and check for damage around the latch and hinges
  9. Windshield and windows: photograph any chips, cracks, or pitting
  10. Interior: photograph seats, dashboard, and cargo area for stains, tears, or damage
  11. Note pre-existing damage on the rental agreement BEFORE leaving the lot:Note pre-existing damage on the rental agreement BEFORE leaving the lot
  12. Repeat the entire walk-around at return while the agent is present:Repeat the entire walk-around at return while the agent is present

Will the rental company accept my photos as evidence?

Yes. Timestamped photos taken at the rental lot with GPS coordinates are strong evidence. The key is taking them BEFORE you accept the keys, when the car is still on the rental lot — that ties the damage to their custody.

What if the rental agent won't note pre-existing damage?

Take a photo of the agent (with permission) or their name tag, and note the date and time of your report. Email a copy of your photos to yourself immediately — the email timestamp is additional evidence that the damage existed at pickup.
