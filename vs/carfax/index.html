What Carfax does well

Carfax aggregates a vehicle's history from thousands of sources — DMV title records, insurance claims, police reports, service records, auction data — into a single report. For a used-car buyer it answers the question every buyer asks: has this car been in a major accident, flooded, salvaged, or odometer-tampered? Carfax is the dominant player in that category and its data depth reflects two decades of source agreements. For pre-purchase diligence, nothing else comes close.

  • Comprehensive accident and title history
  • Service records and odometer readings
  • Recall lookups and structural damage flags
  • Useful as a buyer's diligence tool

Where CarShake wins

CarShake wins the moment the question stops being "what is this car's history?" and becomes "what did this car look like 90 seconds ago, and who has had the keys since?" Carfax cannot answer that. A Carfax report shows whether a car was in an accident three years ago; it says nothing about the scratch on the rear bumper that appeared between when you handed the keys to the valet and when you got the car back. That gap is what CarShake exists to close.

  • Captures condition at the moment of handover, not the car's history
  • Tamper-evident timestamps and QR-confirmed mutual receipt
  • Court-admissible evidence chain for the specific dispute at hand
  • Free for individual drivers (3 scans/mo)

Where Carfax falls short

Carfax is historical, not real-time. It depends on sources reporting in — insurance claims take weeks to appear, service records depend on the shop participating, and minor damage that never triggers a claim never shows up. None of that helps you on the night a valet hands your car back with a fresh dent. Carfax also captures nothing about custody: it cannot tell you who held the keys or when.

  • Historical, not real-time — useless for tonight's dispute
  • No condition capture at handover
  • No custody or handover receipt
  • Minor cosmetic damage often never reported

Our honest verdict

Carfax shows the car's past. CarShake documents its present condition, timestamped. Run Carfax before you buy the car; run CarShake every time you hand the keys to someone else.

Frequently asked questions

Is CarShake a Carfax alternative?

No, complementary. Carfax is history, CarShake is current-state proof.

Should I run Carfax before buying a used car?

Yes — Carfax remains the right tool for pre-purchase diligence on a vehicle's accident and title history. CarShake does not replace that.

Can Carfax help me win a valet damage dispute tonight?

No. Carfax shows historical reported incidents, not tonight's condition. A valet operator can dismiss a Carfax report as irrelevant to a fresh scratch.

Does CarShake pull vehicle history?

No. CarShake captures condition and custody at the moment of handover. It is built for disputes, not for background checks.
