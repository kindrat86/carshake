How to apply this

This guide is not theoretical. The point of understanding learn is to change what you actually do at the moment you hand your car to someone else. The drivers who avoid paying for damage they did not cause all follow the same four-step workflow — and the whole thing takes under 60 seconds.

The 4-step workflow

  1. Before you hand over the keys: open CarShake and run the guided 8-angle scan. The app walks you through each angle so you do not miss high-risk zones like front bumper corners, rear quarter panels, and wheels. Every photo is time-stamped and geo-located automatically.
  2. Generate the QR handover receipt: CarShake produces a QR code the valet, attendant, or service writer can scan to acknowledge the vehicle condition at the moment of transfer. This single step eliminates the vast majority of "the damage was already there" denials.
  3. When you get the car back: run the same 8-angle scan. CarShake compares it to the pre-handover scan and highlights any new damage in a side-by-side diff.
  4. If new damage is present: export the before/after diff with timestamps and geo-data. This is the document you attach to the claim, the dispute letter, or the insurance filing. It is the difference between a he-said-she-said argument and a resolved claim.

Common mistakes

  • Skipping the pre-scan because you are in a hurry. The pre-scan is the entire game. Without it, the post-scan has nothing to compare against and proves nothing. Sixty seconds before you hand over the keys is the highest-leverage minute in your whole trip.
  • Only photographing the obvious sides. Most valet damage happens on front and rear bumpers, corners, and wheels — exactly the spots people skip. The 8-angle guided scan exists precisely to force coverage of these zones.
  • Not getting the receipt scanned. The QR handover receipt is what converts your private photo record into an acknowledged record. Operators who scan it at transfer are dramatically less likely to dispute damage later.
  • Waiting to document damage. Always run the post-scan immediately when you get the car back, while you are still on the property. Waiting until you get home gives the operator room to claim the damage happened in between.

Why this works

The economics are stark. Valet damage disputes are commonly he-said-she-said without photo evidence.
