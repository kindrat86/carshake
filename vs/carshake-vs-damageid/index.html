CarShake vs Damage iD

Damage iD is a hardware-kiosk inspection system for rental fleets and dealerships. CarShake is a consumer app that lives in your pocket.

Damage iD is an enterprise vehicle damage documentation system sold into rental car operators and large dealer groups. Its signature feature is a fixed-installation kiosk — cameras mounted in a drive-through bay or entry lane — that automatically photographs every vehicle driving past and feeds those images into a fleet management workflow. The value proposition is scale: a major airport rental location processing thousands of cars a day cannot have an agent walk around each one with a clipboard. The kiosk captures condition at throughput.

CarShake solves a different problem for a different user. It is a phone app, used by the driver, in the 90 seconds before they hand keys to a valet, rental agent, body shop, or friend. There is no kiosk, no installation, no enterprise contract. The two products are not competitors — they live in entirely different parts of the vehicle's lifecycle.

When to choose CarShake

Choose CarShake if you are the driver, not the operator. The whole product is designed around a single user — the person standing next to their car with a phone, about to hand the keys to someone they do not know. No installation, no contract, no procurement. You download, scan, hand over a QR-coded receipt, and you leave with a timestamped record. If a scratch appears on the bumper when you pick the car up, you have proof it was not there when you handed it over.

CarShake is also the right pick for any handover that happens outside a fixed facility: a hotel valet stand, a restaurant valet, a mechanic's lot, a friend borrowing the car, a concert parking drop-off. Damage iD's kiosk only exists at the rental return lane; it cannot help you at the Ritz-Carlton.

When Damage iD might be better

Damage iD is the right choice for a rental car operator running thousands of vehicles a day through a fixed location. The automated kiosk captures every car at throughput, integrates with the fleet management system, and removes the need for an agent to walk each vehicle. For that specific workflow — high-volume, fixed-site, fleet-side — Damage iD solves a problem CarShake is not built to solve. No phone app can match the throughput of a drive-through camera bay.

Damage iD is also a better fit when the operator's goal is unilateral documentation rather than mutual handover. The kiosk captures condition; it does not need a customer to confirm anything. That is exactly right for the rental return lane and exactly wrong for a valet dispute, where the whole value is the mutually-confirmed receipt.

Frequently asked questions

Is CarShake a cheaper Damage iD?

No — they solve different problems. Damage iD is an enterprise throughput system for fixed rental locations; CarShake is a consumer handover app. Price difference reflects different products, not a discount.

Can a rental operator use both?

Yes. A rental company might run Damage iD at the airport return lane and recommend CarShake to customers for hotel valets, body shop loans, and other handovers that happen outside the kiosk.

Why does Damage iD need a kiosk?

Throughput. A drive-through camera bay can capture thousands of cars a day without an agent walking each one. A phone app cannot match that volume at a single site — but a kiosk cannot follow you to the restaurant valet.

Does CarShake work without installing anything?

CarShake is a phone app. There is no kiosk, no site survey, no integration project. Setup is download and scan.
