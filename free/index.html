What each tool is for

1. AI Car Damage Scanner

The AI Car Damage Scanner lets you upload a single photo of any panel, bumper, wheel, or glass surface and returns a structured list of suspected damage: dent, scratch, scuff, crack, curb rash, paint transfer, or hail impact. Each finding includes a bounding box, an estimated severity (cosmetic / functional / safety), and a confidence score. Use it before a handover to establish a baseline, and again at pickup to see whether anything new appears. Because the analysis runs on-device in your browser, the photo never leaves your phone unless you choose to export it.

2. Car Condition Report Template

A printable, court-friendly condition report with time-stamped fields for all eight standard angles (front, rear, left, right, roof, undercarriage, wheels, and glass), plus driver, location, odometer, and witness fields. Print a stack for your glovebox or fill it on your phone and export as PDF. Operators running valet stands, rental desks, and body-shop intakes use it as the paper fallback when network or device policy forbids browser tools.

3. Damage Photo Guide

The Damage Photo Guide explains exactly how to photograph vehicle damage so the images hold up in a dispute or insurance claim: correct angle and distance per panel, how to include a reference object for scale, why you need wide, mid, and close-up shots of every defect, how to capture reflective surfaces without glare, and how to keep an unbroken metadata chain (timestamp, GPS, device). Follow the guide and even a single scan produces evidence an adjuster will take seriously.

4. Damage Report Generator

The Damage Report Generator turns your photos and notes into a formatted, exportable report with header (date, location, vehicle, parties), per-panel findings table, embedded images, severity ratings, and a summary of total damage count and estimated cost band. Export to PDF for an insurer or to email to a counter-party. It is the fastest way to package a scan into something a claims adjuster, small-claims court, or rental counter will actually read.

5. Rental Car Inspection Checklist

A walk-around rental car inspection checklist for pickup and return: the eighteen inspection points every major rental company uses (bumpers, doors, quarter panels, rocker panels, wheels, windshield, lights, mirrors, roof, trunk, cargo area, seats, dashboard, tires, spare, jack, keys, and fuel), with before-and-after columns so the same sheet travels with the rental. Rental counters routinely charge for pre-existing damage that was never documented at pickup; this checklist is the defence against that charge.

6. Valet Damage Claim Letter

A fill-in-the-blanks valet damage claim letter addressed to the operator, citing the bailment relationship created when keys are handed over, the duty of care owed by the operator, the specific damage found at return, the photographic and time-stamped evidence attached, the repair estimate, and the deadline for response. The letter is written to mirror what consumer-protection attorneys send in pre-suit demand, which is why valet companies and their insurers respond to it rather than ignore it.

7. Valet Dispute Email Template

A copy-paste email template for the moment you discover damage at the valet stand: factual opener, reference to the pre-handover condition, the specific new damage, the evidence attached, a clear ask (repair, credit, manager escalation), and a response window. The tone is firm, professional, and evidence-led, the combination that resolves disputes on the spot instead of escalating to chargebacks or small claims.

8. VIN Check

The VIN Check decodes the 17-character Vehicle Identification Number into year, make, model, plant, trim, engine, and restraint type, then surfaces the vehicle-history signals that matter before a purchase, lease return, or handover: title brand (salvage, rebuilt, flood, odometer rollback flags), recall status, prior theft records, and structural-damage history. Run it on any used car before you sign, and on any rental or loaner before you drive it off the lot.

Which free tool should I use?

Pick the tool by the situation you are standing in:

  • Handing keys to a valet, rental desk, mechanic, or transporter: open the AI Car Damage Scanner for a baseline scan, or print the Car Condition Report Template if you need a paper record both parties can sign.
  • Picking the car back up and something looks wrong: scan again with the AI Car Damage Scanner, then package the findings with the Damage Report Generator.
  • The valet or rental counter is already charging you: send the Valet Dispute Email Template from your phone on the spot, then follow up with the formal Valet Damage Claim Letter.
  • You are buying, leasing, or renting a car and want to know its history: run the VIN Check before you sign.
  • You want to take better evidence photos in any situation: read the Damage Photo Guide first.

What makes vehicle documentation hold up in a dispute

Whether a photo, report, or letter actually defeats a false damage claim comes down to four things, and every free tool on this page is built to deliver them:

  • Contemporaneous timing. Evidence created at the moment of handover is far stronger than evidence created after damage is found. The condition report and AI scanner both produce a time-stamped record you can point to later.
  • Identifiable location. A dispute turns on where the car was and who held the keys. Photos and reports tied to GPS and a named location (garage, hotel, rental desk) anchor the evidence to the responsible party.
  • Mutual confirmation. The single strongest form of proof is a record both sides acknowledged before the dispute existed. The printable condition report has signature fields for both parties; the full CarShake app replaces this with a QR handshake.
  • Unbroken chain. Original files with intact metadata (timestamp, device, location) are admissible; screenshots and re-saved images are not. The Damage Photo Guide walks through exactly how to keep that chain intact from capture to export.

Used together, these free tools cover the full lifecycle of a handover: scan and document before, compare and report after, then dispute in writing if something is wrong. That is the same workflow insurance adjusters and consumer-protection attorneys use, distilled into eight browser tools that cost nothing.
