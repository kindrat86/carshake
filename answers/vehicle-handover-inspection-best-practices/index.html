What should a proper vehicle handover inspection include

Bottom Line A proper vehicle handover inspection should include: exterior scan of all panels (front, rear, sides, roof), wheel and tire condition, glass condition, interior overview, and a time-stamped digital receipt — all captured before the vehicle leaves your possession and after it returns.

Exterior Panel Scan

Photograph all four sides including bumpers, doors, fenders, and mirrors. Pay special attention to low areas (below the beltline) where parking lot damage most commonly occurs. A systematic walk-around pattern (front → passenger side → rear → driver side) ensures nothing is missed.

Wheels, Tires, and Glass

Curb rash on wheels and windshield chips are the two most common valet-damage disputes. Take close-up photos of all four wheels and the windshield. These are expensive repairs ($500-1,500 for a single wheel, $300-800 for windshield replacement) and the most frequently contested.

Interior Condition

At minimum, photograph the driver's seat, steering wheel, dashboard, and center console. For rental and fleet vehicles, also capture the rear seats and cargo area. Interior damage (rips, stains, odors) can be as costly as exterior damage and is harder to prove without documentation.

The Digital Receipt Standard

A proper inspection must produce a digital receipt that records: date and time, GPS location, photo count, vehicle identifier (plate or VIN), and a unique verification code. This receipt is what transforms a set of photos into a defensible inspection record.

Fleet and Commercial Handovers

For fleets and commercial operations, add: odometer reading, fuel level, maintenance indicators, and any pre-existing damage noted by the previous driver. Consistent documentation across all handovers creates an auditable fleet history that reduces dispute resolution time from weeks to minutes.

Key Data Points

  • 4-point inspection protocol
  • Systematic walk-around pattern
  • Digital receipt with verification code

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How long should a handover inspection take?

    A thorough scan takes 2-3 minutes. It's the most valuable 2-3 minutes you'll spend before handing over your keys — potentially saving thousands in disputed damage claims.

  • Can I use CarShake for rental cars?

    Yes. It's ideal for rental cars. Scan before you drive off the lot and again when you return. If the rental company claims damage, you have time-stamped evidence of the car's condition at both points.
