How to Dispute Valet Damage and Win

Complete guide to disputing damage caused by valet parking services.

Valet damage is frustrating and often denied by the parking company. But with the right documentation and approach, you can win your dispute and get compensated.

Step 1: Document Immediately

Before leaving the valet area, inspect your car thoroughly. Take photos of any new damage from multiple angles. Use CarShake to create a timestamped condition report on the spot.

Step 2: Report to the Valet Manager

Report the damage to the valet manager before leaving the premises. Get their name and a written incident report. Do not leave without filing a report.

Step 3: File a Claim

File a claim with the valet company's insurance. Include your photos, the incident report, repair estimates, and any witness statements.

Step 4: Escalate If Needed

If the valet company denies responsibility, file with your own insurance (they will subrogate), file a complaint with the Better Business Bureau, and consider small claims court.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is responsible for valet damage?

The valet company is legally responsible for damage caused by their employees while your car is in their custody. However, they often deny responsibility, which is why documentation is critical.
