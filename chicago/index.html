The problem is universal: when a valet damages your car, proving it happened during their custody is extremely difficult without timestamped before-and-after photos. Rental return disputes, hotel valet scrapes, and restaurant parking lot incidents all come down to your word against theirs. CarShake eliminates that ambiguity.

How Chicago valet damage law works

Illinois applies a bailment framework where commercial valets are strictly liable for damages to vehicles in their custody during the bailment period.

In practice, this means the valet company is not automatically liable. You have to prove the damage occurred while they had control of your vehicle. That requires evidence: photos taken immediately before handover, a timestamped receipt, and ideally photos at pickup showing any new damage. Most Chicago drivers do not have this evidence, which is exactly why valet damage claims get denied.

Using CarShake at Chicago venues
