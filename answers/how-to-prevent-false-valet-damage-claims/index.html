How can drivers protect themselves from false valet damage claims

Bottom Line The most reliable protection against false valet damage claims is a time-stamped, geotagged photo scan of the vehicle's condition at handover and pickup — ideally with a QR-coded receipt that serves as court-admissible proof.

The Problem: He-Said-She-Said

Without documented evidence of a car's condition before handover, valet damage disputes almost always favor the valet company. Their insurance and legal teams handle these daily; individual drivers don't stand a chance. A single disputed claim can cost thousands in repairs and increased premiums.

Time-Stamped Photo Scans

Taking photos isn't enough — you need time-stamped, geo-tagged scans that establish exactly when and where the vehicle was inspected. Courts and insurance companies require metadata-provable evidence. CarShake captures this automatically with every scan.

The Before-and-After Handover Protocol

The gold standard: scan the vehicle's exterior condition at handover (before), receive a QR-coded receipt, then scan again at pickup (after). Any damage that appears in the after scan but not the before scan is provably not your responsibility. This creates an unbroken chain of custody.

Why QR-Coded Receipts Matter

A QR-coded handover receipt links to a tamper-evident record with photos, timestamps, and GPS coordinates. This is the evidence standard that courts and insurance adjusters accept. Verbal agreements and unverified photos get dismissed — a QR receipt with metadata is court-admissible.

Who Benefits Most

Drivers using valet services, rental car customers, fleet operators managing multiple vehicles, car dealerships handling test drives, body shops receiving customer vehicles, and parking facilities — anyone who hands over or receives a vehicle benefits from documented condition proof.
