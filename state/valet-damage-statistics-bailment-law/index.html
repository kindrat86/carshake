What is bailment law?

Bailment is the legal relationship created when you hand your car keys to a valet. You (the bailor) transfer temporary custody of your property. The valet (the bailee) accepts that custody and owes you a duty of reasonable care. This is the foundation of every valet damage claim in the United States.

In a bailment, when the car is returned damaged, the burden shifts: the bailee must explain why the damage occurred. But the initial burden — proving the car's condition before the bailment — still falls on the driver. Without timestamped before-state photographs, the valet's defense ("it was already like that") is nearly impossible to refute.
