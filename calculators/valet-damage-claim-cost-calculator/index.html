How valet damage claims work

When a valet damages your car, the claim process typically follows four steps: (1) you notice the damage and report it — ideally before leaving the premises; (2) the valet company's insurance is notified; (3) an adjuster evaluates the damage; (4) a settlement is reached. At every step, the burden of proof is on YOU to show the damage wasn't pre-existing.

Why valet companies fight these claims

Valet operators and their insurers deny roughly 60–70% of initial damage claims. Their standard defense: "the damage was pre-existing." The deductible trap

Even if your insurance covers valet damage under comprehensive or collision coverage, you still pay your deductible — typically $500–$1,000. And filing a claim can raise your premium by 20–40% for 3–5 years. A $1,500 repair with a $500 deductible and 3 years of $300/year premium increases means you're paying $1,900 out of pocket.
