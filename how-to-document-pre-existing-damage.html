How to Document Pre-Existing Car Damage

Every valet, mechanic, and car wash will check for damage when you hand over your car. If they find a scratch you cannot prove was already there — you pay for it. Here is how to document pre-existing damage so you never pay for someone else's mistake.

1. Take 8-angle photos before every handover

Photograph the car from: front, rear, driver side, passenger side, front-left quarter, front-right quarter, rear-left quarter, rear-right quarter. Stand 6 feet away for context, then move in close for detail shots of any existing damage. Timestamps and geotags are essential — they prove when and where the photos were taken.

2. Record a walkaround video

A 30-second video walking around the car captures the flow of damage that still photos miss. Narrate as you walk: "Front bumper — no damage. Driver side — small scratch on rear door, pre-existing. Rear bumper — clean." This creates a verbal record alongside the visual.

3. Use a digital inspection tool with timestamp proof

CarShake automates this entire process: 8-angle AI scan, automatic timestamps, geotags, and a QR receipt you can share. The scan takes 30 seconds and creates a court-admissible record that defeats false damage claims before they start.

4. Get the attendant to sign off

Before handing over your keys, show the attendant your pre-existing damage documentation. Ask them to acknowledge it — most will note it on the ticket. This creates a second witness to the vehicle's pre-existing condition and makes it much harder to claim the damage happened during their custody.

The most common pre-existing damage types

Door dings ($150-$500), bumper scratches ($300-$1,200), rim curb rash ($100-$400), paint chips from road debris ($100-$500). These are the most common pre-existing conditions that valet companies try to blame on their attendants. Document them before every handover.
