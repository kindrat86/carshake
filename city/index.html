Why City-Specific Valet Knowledge Matters

Valet parking isn't the same experience in every city. In New York, valet services operate under strict NYC Administrative Code requirements with mandatory insurance minimums. In Las Vegas, casino-resorts process over 50,000 valet handoffs per day, creating a high-volume ecosystem where damage disputes are common. In Los Angeles, valet parking is pervasive at restaurants, hotels, and medical centers — so much so that the city has specific zoning ordinances governing valet operations.

Each city has its own combination of state bailment laws, local ordinances, insurance requirements, and practical factors — traffic density, weather patterns, parking infrastructure — that affect your risk of valet damage and your legal recourse if it happens. CarShake's city guides break down what you need to know for 40+ US cities, from the legal framework to practical tips for documenting your car before handing over the keys.

What every city guide covers: the applicable state bailment law and how it applies to valet parking, the statute of limitations for filing a claim, what to do immediately after discovering damage, how to use CarShake's GPS-verified photo evidence in your specific city, and city-specific tips based on local parking infrastructure and valet density.

How CarShake Works in Any City

Regardless of which city you're in, CarShake's protection process is the same. Before you hand over your keys at any valet stand, restaurant, hotel, airport, or event — open CarShake on your phone's web browser (no app download needed), follow the guided 60-second photo capture process, and let CarShake automatically GPS-tag and dual-timestamp every image. When you return, repeat the scan. CarShake's AI compares the before and after images instantly, flagging any new damage that appeared while your car was out of your possession.

Every photo record is SHA-256 hashed and stored immutably, with dual independent timestamps from your device and CarShake's servers. This creates a chain of evidence that satisfies the evidentiary standards of any city's small claims court, making it extremely difficult for valet operators to dispute your claim. And it's completely free — CarShake's core valet protection features have no cost, no subscription, and no app download required.
