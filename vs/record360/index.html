CarShake vs Record360

Record360 is a rental-condition inspection app for operators. CarShake is a handover protocol for drivers.

Record360 is a tablet-based condition inspection app used primarily by equipment rental yards, car rental operators, and dealerships. An agent walks around the asset, photographs damage against a checklist, records narration, and produces a time-stamped PDF that becomes the official record of condition at handover. It is well-engineered for the operator's problem: protect the rental company from false damage claims by capturing condition on the way in and on the way out. Enterprise customers include United Rentals and other heavy-equipment fleets.

CarShake approaches the same moment from the other side of the counter. It is a phone app the customer uses, not the agent. The three-stop protocol — scan before, QR handover receipt, scan after — is built around mutual confirmation at the moment of transfer, which is the evidentiary gap that purely operator-side tools like Record360 leave open.

When to choose CarShake

Pick CarShake if you are the one handing the keys over and you want your own copy of the evidence. The asymmetry that Record360 leaves open is control: the operator captures condition, the operator stores the report, and the customer gets a copy only if the operator chooses to share it. If a damage dispute ever turns into a he-said-she-said, the customer is arguing against the operator's own record. CarShake flips that. You scan on your phone, you hold the timestamped record, and the QR-coded receipt is a shared artifact both parties acknowledged at the moment of transfer.

CarShake also wins outside the rental counter. Record360's value collapses the moment the asset is not a rental vehicle sitting on the operator's lot — a hotel valet, a body shop loaner, a friend borrowing your car, a concert valet. CarShake's protocol travels with the driver, not the facility.

When Record360 might be better

Record360 is the right call if you are the operator running a rental or fleet business and you need a standardized, agent-driven inspection workflow across hundreds or thousands of handovers per week. The checklist engine, narration, signature capture, and integration with rental management software solve a back-office problem CarShake does not touch. Heavy-equipment rental yards, in particular, need the depth of asset-condition documentation that Record360 was built for.

Record360 is also the stronger choice when the operator's workflow is fundamentally one-sided — capture condition, file the report, move on. The QR handover receipt that makes CarShake valuable in a dispute is overhead in a throughput-driven rental yard that processes a car every two minutes.

Frequently asked questions

Is CarShake competing with Record360?

No. Record360 is operator-side inspection software; CarShake is a customer-side handover protocol. A rental customer can use CarShake even when the operator is running Record360, and the two records simply document the same moment from opposite sides of the counter.

Does Record360 produce a QR handover receipt?

No. Record360 produces a signed PDF condition report, held and distributed by the operator. There is no mutually-confirmed receipt tying condition to the exact moment of key transfer.

Why would I use CarShake if the rental company already documented the car?

Because the rental company's record is controlled by the rental company. CarShake gives you, the customer, an independent timestamped copy — and the QR receipt proves both parties saw the same condition at the same moment.

Is CarShake free to use for a single rental?

Yes. The free tier includes three scans per month, which covers a typical rental pickup and return. Shield+ ($2.97/month) is for frequent travelers and rideshare drivers.
