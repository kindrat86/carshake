How to Dispute a Valet Damage Claim (Step by Step)

The average valet damage claim is valet damage. Most drivers lose these disputes because they lack evidence — not because the damage did not happen. Here is the exact process to win your dispute.

Step 1: Document the damage immediately

Take timestamped photos from multiple angles before leaving the valet stand. Capture the damage up close (within 2 feet) and from 6 feet away to show location context. If possible, include the valet ticket or receipt in one of the photos — this ties the damage to the specific handover.

Do NOT drive away and photograph later. Once you leave the premises, the valet company will claim the damage happened after you left. The photos must be timestamped and geotagged at the valet location.

Step 2: Get the valet manager immediately

Do not speak only to the valet attendant who parked your car. Ask for the shift manager or supervisor. State calmly: "My car has damage that was not present when I handed over the keys. I need to file an incident report right now."

If the manager is not available, get the name of the valet company (not just the venue), the attendant's name, and the manager's contact information. Most valet stands are operated by third-party companies, not the venue itself.

Step 3: File a written incident report

Demand a written incident report on the spot. Do not accept a verbal assurance that "we will look into it." The report should include: date and time, vehicle make and model, description of damage, names of staff involved, and your contact information. Take a photo of the completed report before leaving.

Step 4: Escalate to the valet company's insurance

Most valet companies carry garage-keepers liability insurance. Request the insurance carrier name and policy number. If the manager refuses, contact the venue (hotel/restaurant) directly — they hold the contract with the valet company and can provide this information. Send a formal demand letter with photos attached within 48 hours.

Step 5: Use timestamped evidence as leverage

The single strongest piece of evidence in a valet damage dispute is a timestamped scan of the vehicle's condition before handover. A tool like CarShake captures 8-angle scans with geotags and timestamps, creating court-admissible proof of the vehicle's condition at the moment you handed over the keys.

Without a pre-handover scan, the dispute becomes your word against theirs. With one, the evidence speaks for itself. Most claims settle within 72 hours when timestamped proof is presented.

FAQ

What if the valet company denies the damage?

Request their insurance information and file directly with their carrier. If they refuse, file a claim with your own insurance and provide all evidence — your insurer will subrogate against the valet company's policy.

How long do I have to file a valet damage claim?

Most valet companies require incident reports within 24 hours. Insurance claims typically must be filed within 30 days. Report immediately — delay weakens your case.
