What vehicle damage actually costs

The real cost of a damage dispute is almost never just the repair bill. Drivers focus on the sticker price of the dent repair and miss the cascade of costs that follow: the deductible on your insurance claim, the time off work to get multiple estimates, the hours on the phone with the valet company's insurance, the rental car while yours is in the shop, the diminished-value hit when you eventually sell or trade in the car, and the stress tax of fighting a denial for weeks. Add it all up and the average valet damage claim runs roughly valet damage — and a single disputed claim, even one you ultimately win, costs $500+ out of pocket.

The cost breakdown

  • Repair cost: the actual body work. Bumper corner damage from a valet typically runs $400-$900. Wheel curb rash runs $150-$400 per wheel. Door dings $75-$250.
  • Insurance deductible: typically $250-$1,000, paid out of pocket before coverage kicks in.
    • Diminished value: a car with a damage history on its Carfax sells for less. Even after a perfect repair, a late-model car can lose $1,000-$3,000 in resale value.
  • Time cost: getting three estimates, filing the claim, fighting the denial, renting a replacement car. Realistically 8-20 hours of your time.
  • Stress and hassle: unquantifiable but real. Most drivers give up on legitimate claims because the fight is not worth it.
