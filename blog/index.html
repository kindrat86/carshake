Frequently Asked Questions

How does CarShake prove vehicle damage before valet parking?

CarShake runs a three-stop protocol: you scan the car before handing over the keys, generate a QR-coded handover receipt, then scan again on retrieval. Each scan captures timestamped, geotagged photos that document the exact condition of the vehicle. That evidence chain is court-admissible and settles false damage claims fast, protecting both the driver and the valet operator.

What does CarShake cost for a valet operator?

CarShake is free for individual drivers who want to document a single handover. Valet operators, rental fleets, and dealerships use paid plans that add unlimited scans, team accounts, branded receipts, and audit-trail exports. Pricing scales by monthly handover volume, so a small hotel valet stand pays far less than a busy airport parking operation processing thousands of cars.

Why do valet businesses get hit with false damage claims?

A customer retrieves their car, spots a scratch they never noticed before, and blames the valet. Without timestamped proof of pre-existing condition, the operator usually pays. Studies of parking operations show damage disputes are a top source of chargebacks and insurance friction. CarShake removes the ambiguity by fixing the vehicle's condition in verifiable photos at both handover points.

Can CarShake evidence be used in an insurance dispute?

Yes. Every CarShake scan is stamped with a date, time, and location, and the photos are stored immutably so they cannot be edited after the fact. Insurers and small-claims courts accept this kind of contemporaneous, tamper-evident record. Operators simply export the handover receipt as a PDF and attach it to the claim, turning a he-said-she-said dispute into a documented fact.

What CarShake Is

CarShake is a free, no-download web app that creates court-ready proof of your vehicle's condition before and after you hand over the keys. You scan your car in about 60 seconds — the camera captures eight angles into timestamped, geotagged photos — then generate a QR handover receipt that the valet, attendant, or service advisor scans to co-sign the car's condition. When you get the car back, you re-scan, and CarShake's AI compares every angle to flag any new scratch, dent, curb rash, or scrape. Each record is SHA-256 verified, so it can't be edited after the fact. The result: when a false damage claim appears, you hold tamper-evident evidence instead of arguing "how do we know it wasn't already like that?"

More Frequently Asked Questions

Is CarShake really free?

Yes — documenting your own handover as an individual driver is free, with no app to download. You open CarShake in your phone browser and scan. Paid plans exist for valet operators, rental fleets, and dealerships that need unlimited scans, team accounts, and audit-trail exports.

Do I need to download an app?

No. CarShake runs in your mobile browser, so there is nothing to install and nothing to update. That matters at the valet stand, where you have seconds — you just open the page and scan.

How long does a scan take?

About 60 seconds. You walk around the car once while CarShake guides you to capture eight angles. The whole three-stop protocol — scan, QR handover, re-scan — adds roughly three minutes to a handover you were doing anyway.

What is bailment law, and how does it protect me?

When you hand your keys to a valet, you create a legal "bailment": the operator takes temporary custody of your property and owes a duty of care to return it in the same condition. The problem is proof. Bailment only helps you if you can show the condition at handover — which is exactly the record CarShake creates.

Does the valet or attendant have to scan the QR code?

Co-signing with the QR receipt is the strongest form of proof because both parties confirm the condition. If an attendant declines, your timestamped, geotagged before-scan still stands on its own as contemporaneous evidence — you are never worse off for having scanned.

What happens if CarShake finds new damage at pickup?

The AI comparison flags each new scratch, dent, or scrape with the before-and-after photos side by side. You export the handover receipt as a PDF and use it on the spot to dispute the charge, or attach it to an insurance or small-claims filing. That turns a he-said-she-said argument into a documented fact.
