QR handover receipt, explained

A QR handover receipt is a tamper-evident digital record that fixes the exact moment legal custody of a vehicle transfers from one party to another. The receipt contains a QR code that, when scanned, retrieves the full condition record captured immediately before handover along with a server-side timestamp proving when the transfer occurred. CarShake generates one automatically as the second stop of the three-stop protocol.

The receipt solves a specific evidentiary problem that traditional valet tickets create. Paper tickets carry a ticket number and the word "not responsible" but no condition record and no verifiable timestamp. They are easy to lose, easy to forge, and worthless as evidence in a dispute. A QR handover receipt, by contrast, is cryptographically linked to the pre-handover scan, cannot be altered after generation, and can be retrieved by anyone with the code — the owner, the operator, the insurer, or the court.

The receipt's value comes from what it proves. In a damage dispute, the operator's most common defense is "the damage occurred after handover." A QR handover receipt defeats that defense by establishing a precise, neutral, third-party-stamped moment of transfer. Any damage present in the post-retrieval scan but absent from the pre-handover scan must have occurred between those two timestamps — during the operator's custody.

Why QR handover receipt matters

Custody is the central concept in bailment law. The party holding the vehicle is the party responsible for it. Without a verifiable record of when custody transferred, the operator can plausibly deny that it ever accepted custody at all, or claim the vehicle was returned earlier than it actually was. The QR receipt eliminates that ambiguity.

The receipt also matters operationally. Valet stands process hundreds of vehicles per night. A paper-ticket system relies on an attendant matching the right ticket to the right car, which fails constantly. A QR code is scanned, not matched, and the link to the condition record is automatic. The receipt also creates a shared record that both the owner and the operator can reference if a dispute arises later.

How CarShake handles this

CarShake generates a QR handover receipt at the moment the owner hands over the keys. The receipt links to the pre-handover scan, is timestamped server-side, and is stored in a way that neither party can alter. When the vehicle is returned, scanning the same QR code retrieves the full handover record and triggers the post-retrieval scan to complete the three-stop protocol.

Frequently asked questions

What is a QR handover receipt?

A tamper-evident digital record, generated by CarShake, that fixes the exact moment a vehicle's custody transfers and links to the condition record captured immediately before.

Is a paper valet ticket still required?

Some venues still issue paper tickets alongside the QR receipt, but the QR receipt is the authoritative record. Paper tickets carry no condition information and no verifiable timestamp.

Who can scan the QR code?

Both the owner and the operator. The receipt is designed to be a shared, neutral record that both parties can reference in the event of a dispute.
