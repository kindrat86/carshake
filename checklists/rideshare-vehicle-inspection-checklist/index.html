Rideshare Driver Vehicle Inspection Checklist: Protect Your Livelihood

Your car is your office. A weekly documented inspection catches issues before they become breakdowns, and a timestamped condition record protects you when passengers or parking lots cause damage you did not notice until later.

  1. Exterior: photograph all four sides, bumpers, wheels, and windows for new damage
  2. Interior: check seats, floor mats, and door panels for passenger-caused damage or stains
  3. Dashboard: verify no new warning lights, check odometer and fuel level
  4. Tires: check tread depth and pressure (rideshare driving accelerates tire wear)
  5. Lights: verify headlights, taillights, brake lights, and turn signals all work
  6. Fluids: check oil, coolant, brake fluid, and windshield washer levels
  7. Documentation: note the date, mileage, and any new issues found
  8. Save the inspection record — it is your evidence when a passenger or parking lot causes damage:Save the inspection record — it is your evidence when a passenger or parking lot causes damage

How often should I inspect my rideshare vehicle?

Weekly for the full checklist. Daily for a quick walk-around (30 seconds: check for new exterior damage, verify no warning lights, glance at tires). The more often you document, the stronger your evidence when a dispute arises.

What if a passenger damages my car?

Report it to the rideshare platform immediately, file a police report if it was intentional, and use your timestamped pre-ride inspection as evidence of the car's prior condition.

Complete rideshare vehicle inspection checklist

This checklist covers every step you should take when handling vehicle documentation at a rideshare. Following it systematically protects you against damage disputes, liability claims, and undocumented charges.

Step-by-step checklist

  1. Complete annual mechanical inspection
  2. Verify all lights, signals, and brakes function
  3. Check tire tread depth (minimum 4/32 inch)
  4. Ensure interior is clean and presentable
  5. Scan exterior condition with CarShake daily
  6. Document any damage after each shift
  7. Keep maintenance records current
  8. Verify rideshare platform inspection requirements

Why each step matters

Skip a step, and you create a gap in your documentation that can be exploited later. For example, if you do not photograph the odometer at drop-off, a rideshare can claim excessive mileage. If you do not scan the condition at handover, pre-existing damage may be attributed to you.

CarShake automates the critical photo-documentation steps. Instead of manually managing photos and notes, the app walks you through each panel and timestamp, ensuring nothing is missed.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Rushing through inspection: Take the full 5-10 minutes. Rushing leads to missed damage.
  • Signing without inspecting: Never sign a condition acknowledgment without verifying every detail.
  • Skipping photos of minor damage: Small scratches become major disputes. Document everything.
  • Not getting signatures: Verbal agreements are not evidence. Get written acknowledgment.
  • Forgetting the interior: Interior damage is frequently disputed. Photograph seats, dash, and cargo areas.

Pro tips from claims adjusters

  • Use CarShake's timestamped photos as your primary evidence layer.
  • Always get the name and contact info of the person receiving your vehicle.
  • Keep copies of all paperwork, originals get lost.
  • Follow up any verbal communication with an email summary.
  • If damage appears at pickup, document it before leaving the rideshare.

What if the rideshare staff will not wait for me to inspect?
