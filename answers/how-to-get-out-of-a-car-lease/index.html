How can you get out of a car lease without paying early termination fees

Bottom Line The most cost-effective way to exit a car lease is a lease transfer: find someone to take over your remaining payments. This avoids early termination fees (typically $2,000-5,000), excess mileage charges, and negative credit impact.

Option 1: Lease Transfer (Best Option)

Find someone to assume your lease. They take over your remaining monthly payments and the car. You walk away with no fees. Lease transfer marketplaces connect you with qualified buyers. Total cost: $0-500 for the transfer to process.

Option 2: Early Buyout + Resell

Buy out your lease from the finance company, then sell the car privately or to a dealer. This works when your car's market value exceeds the buyout price. You may even make money if used car prices are high.

Option 3: Lease Trade-In

Some dealers will buy out your lease as part of a trade-in toward a new vehicle. They handle the paperwork. You may get less value than a private transaction, but it's the simplest process.

Option 4: Early Termination (Last Resort)

Return the car to the dealer and pay the early termination fee. This typically costs $2,000-5,000 plus any excess mileage and wear charges. Only use this if you need to exit immediately and other options aren't viable.

What NOT to Do

Don't stop making payments — this damages your credit for 7 years. Don't let someone 'take over' informally without a proper lease transfer through the finance company. You remain legally responsible if they stop paying.

Key Data Points

  • $2,000-5,000 saved vs termination
  • 4 exit strategies ranked
  • $0-500 lease transfer cost

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Does lease transfer affect my credit?

    No. A proper lease transfer through your finance company removes your name from the lease and the new lessee assumes full responsibility. Your credit is unaffected as long as all payments were current at the time of transfer.

  • Can I transfer a lease with bad credit?

    The new lessee must qualify for the lease through the finance company's credit check. Your credit doesn't matter — only the person taking over the lease needs to qualify.
