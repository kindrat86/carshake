How to File an Insurance Claim for Valet Damage

Step-by-step process for filing insurance claims after valet damage.

If the valet company refuses to pay for damage, your insurance can help. Here is the process.

Step 1: Gather Documentation

You need: photos of the damage (before and after if possible), the valet ticket, the incident report, repair estimates, and any communication with the valet company.

Step 2: File With the Valet Company First

Always file with the valet company's insurance first. If they accept responsibility, your insurance does not need to be involved and your rates will not be affected.

Step 3: File With Your Insurance

If the valet company denies the claim, file with your own insurance. They will investigate and may subrogate (recover the cost from the valet company's insurance).

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my insurance rates go up from a valet damage claim?

If the damage is the valet company's fault and your insurance subrogates successfully, your rates should not increase. However, if you file through collision coverage, it may affect your rates depending on your insurer.
