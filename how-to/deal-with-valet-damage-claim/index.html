How to Deal With a Valet Damage Claim: A Fair Process for Operators and Drivers

Quick answer: If you run a valet operation or work as a valet driver, damage claims from customers are a cost of doing business — but not every claim is legitimate. Here is a fair, documented process for handling them that protects both your business and your customers.

Published 2026-07-09

Step 1: Ask for the customer's evidence first

The single most important question: does the customer have timestamped documentation of the car's condition at drop-off? If they do, the claim is likely legitimate and should be resolved promptly. If they do not, the claim requires more investigation.

Step 2: Check your incident logs and camera footage

Did your team note any pre-existing damage at intake? Many valet operations use intake forms or digital check-in that already captures vehicle condition. Security cameras may show whether the damage was present at drop-off.

Step 3: Respond in writing within the customer's deadline

If you receive a formal demand letter, respond — even if the answer is 'we are investigating.' Ignoring a demand letter is the fastest way to end up in small-claims court. A prompt written response keeps the conversation out of court.

Am I legally required to pay every valet damage claim?

No. But if the customer has contemporaneous evidence (timestamped photos at handover) and you cannot explain the damage, bailment law strongly favors the customer. Paying legitimate claims quickly is cheaper than fighting and losing in court.
